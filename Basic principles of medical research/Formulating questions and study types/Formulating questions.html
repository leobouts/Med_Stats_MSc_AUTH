    Formulating questions

    Let us, all together, try to formulate questions on the following clinical scenarios:

     

    1. A 43-year old patient visits you at your office for the renewal of a prescription. He asks you if there are any preventive measures he could apply in order to avoid an episode of deep vein thrombosis, during a 12-h flight to South America that he is going to have next week. He is deeply concerned, as his brother had such an event the previous year. You propose elastic socks, as the most efficient intervention.

     

    2. You examine a patient with heart failure that underwent myocardial infarction 6 weeks ago. He has an uneventful recovery, with no dyspnea, 80 beats/min, blood pressure of 136/85 mm Hg and normal findings on pulmonary ausculation. Nevertheless, heart ultrasound examination reveals diminished cardiac function, with left ventricular ejection fraction (LVEF) of 30%. You wonder if the prescription of a beta-blocker would be a safe and efficient approach.

     

    3. Maria (35 year-old) is pregnant, during the first trimester. She is concerned about the possibility of trisomy 21 and she has heard of amniocentesis and nuchal transparency scan as diagnostic methods for the syndrome. She wonders whether the new, non-invasive procedure is equally reliable to the classic amniocentesis.

     

    4. In the Emergency Department, you examine a patient with a clinical picture of acute cholecystitis. For the confirmation of the diagnosis, a computed tomography (CT) has been prescribed. Nevertheless, the resident suggests that abdominal ultrasound has superior diagnostic value compared to CT for the diagnosis of acute cholecystitis.

     

    5. A 73-year old woman with osteoarthritis and arterial hypertension visits you at your office together with her daughter. The patient complains of severe knee pain. Her daughter asks for a prescription of the new analgesics [cyclo-oxygenase-2 (COX-2) inhibitors], as she has heard that they may reduce the risk of gastrointestinal bleeding.

     

    Please, start to formulate clinical questions or try to improve the ones suggested by your colleagues.

    Measures of association with R

    Example in R

    Risk is the probability of an event happening or not happening in a defined population over a specified time interval. Examine a 2-by-2 cross tabulation of exposure and disease:

    Disease

    No disease

    Total

    Exposed

    a

    b

    a+b

    Not exposed

    c

    d

    c+d

    Total

    a+c

    b+d

    a+b+c+d


    The risk for exposed individuals is expressed as a/(a+b); similarly, the risk for not exposed individuals is c/(c+d).  

    The risk ratio (or relative risk) is the ratio of the proportion of the exposed that develop the disease to the proportion of the unexposed that develop it: 

    • The risk ratio requires complete follow-up of data since the calculation of risk is based on the population at risk at the start of the study
    • The risk ratio doesn't account for time to event between groups, only final outcome
    • The risk ratio is most appropriate to assess protective effects of an intervention

    Risk ratios with more than two levels

    Blood type

    Not transfused

    Transfused

    Risk

    A

    62

    33

    33/(62 + 33) = 0.38

    AB

    9

    3

    3/(3 + 9) = 0.25

    B

    15

    18

    18/(15 + 18) = 0.55

    O

    65

    46

    46/(65 + 46) = 0.41


    The risk for a patient with blood type A to be transfused is 0.38. For blood type AB the risk falls to 0.25, for B it reaches 0.55 and for O it is 0.41. Using the O group as reference we can calculate the risk ratio for types A, AB and B. To interpret the results keep in mind that the value in the denominator is the reference group; that is, we examine the other groups against it. 

    • The risk ratio for A group compared to O group is 0.38/0.41 = 0.92 which indicates that the risk of transfusion in type A is 0.92 times the risk in type O. This means that the risk is lower in type A since the risk ratio is < 1.
    • The risk ratio for AB group compared to O group is 0.25/0.41 = 0.61. Again the risk ratio is < 1 so the risk in AB type is lower than the risk in type O.
    • The risk ratio for B group compared to O group is 0.55/0.41 = 1.34. This time the risk is > 1. So the risk of transfusion in type B is 1.34 times the risk in type O.

    The value in both the above functions includes:

    • The $data value: the table used for calculations with marginal totals
    • The $measure value: the ratio (risk or odds) and confidence intervals
    • The $p.value: only used for the test of independence
    • The $correction: information on whether continuity correction was used (TRUE or FALSE)
